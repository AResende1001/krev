Complete Krev Dictionary

1. Fundamental Structure and Grammar

1.1. Rigid Sentence Order (SVO - Subject-Verb-Object)

The standard order is **Subject (P) + Verb (V) + Object (N)**. Verbs are never conjugated.

Mi fren Den Mets. (I eat the Meat.)

1.2. Negation

Use the particle Na after the Subject and before the Verb.

Vi Na sleep. (You do Not sleep.)

1.3. Possession (Genitive)

Use the particle De. The structure is: **[Item Possessed] De [Possessor]**.

Den Dom De Mi. (The House Of Me / My House.)

1.4. Comparative

Use the structure **[Linking Verb] + Ple [Adjective] Zan**.

Den Liv zek Ple Za Zan Den Dom. (The Book is More Big Than the House.)

1.5. Question (Q) and Tense/Aspect Particles (Ke, Lo)

Aspect and Question particles come at the beginning of the sentence, in priority order: **[Aspect/Tense] + [Q (if it's a question)]**.

Ke Q vi fren Den Mets? (Did you eat the Meat?)

Lo mi lo sleep. (I will sleep.)

1.6. Absolute Transparency Rule

Krev strictly prohibits the existence of Homonyms, Homographs, Homophones, and Paronyms. Every word must have one unique and unambiguous meaning.

1.7. Capitalization (Orthography)

Nouns (N), Adjectives (ADJ), and Functional Particles (Tense, Question, Possession, Articles, Conjunctions, Numbers) are **always capitalized**. Pronouns (P), Verbs (V), and Adverbs (ADV) are only capitalized if they start the sentence.

4. Philosophy and Biography of the Krev Language

Krev is a **Hybrid Constructed Language (Conlang)** created with the goal of facilitating communication. It is in **constant and dynamic development**, aiming to maximize **efficiency** and **clarity** in daily use. Its current structure is a simplified synthesis of global influences, mixing simple grammatical rules with familiar vocabulary derived from various source languages.

Core Bases and Influences

  • Absolute Transparency Rule: Krev's fundamental principle is clarity. The language is built to contain no homonyms, homographs, homophones, or paronyms, ensuring every word has a unique and unambiguous meaning, eliminating reliance on context for comprehension.
  • Indo-European Vocabulary: Most of the central and abstract lexicon is derived from **Germanic** (English, German) and **Romance** (Latin, Spanish, Portuguese) roots, making Krev accessible and familiar to speakers of these families.
  • Minimalist Grammar: The grammatical structure eliminates verb conjugation, grammatical gender, and most case inflections. Tenses and aspect are marked by particles at the start of the sentence (**Ke, Lo**), maximizing order and transparency.
  • Rigid SVO Syntax: The rigid word order of **Subject-Verb-Object (SVO)**, along with functional particles, is inspired by the clarity and lack of ambiguity found in many Creole languages.

Krev was conceived as a practical language, capable of expressing everything from philosophical concepts (Ver - Truth) to daily necessities (Morlan - Breakfast) with the minimum of rules and exceptions.
